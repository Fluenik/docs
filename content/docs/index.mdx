BRAM sits between your AI agents and the rest of your stack. It gives product, engineering, and compliance teams one place to:

- **Govern** — define policies that apply at prompt, response, and tool-call boundaries, and enforce them in real time.
- **Observe** — capture every AI interaction (queries, answers, tool calls, retrievals) with full context and audit-grade logging.
- **Evaluate** — run adversarial and behavioural evaluations across your agents, from one-off runtime checks to dataset-driven regressions.

## How it's different

BRAM is **framework-agnostic** and **model-agnostic**. If your agent speaks MCP, BRAM can govern it. If your application calls an LLM, it can call our Evals API. You keep the model provider you already use (Anthropic, OpenAI, Azure OpenAI, AWS Bedrock, open-source) — BRAM is the governance layer on top.
